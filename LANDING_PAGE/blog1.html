Tug-of-War with C++ and HTML/CSS

By: Aica Ortega

Nakaranas na ba kayo na parang sasabog na ang utak niyo dahil sa dami ng keywords at syntax na kailangang tandaan? Switching from object-oriented logic to visual design feels like a constant reality check that I wasn’t fully prepared for. It’s amazing yet honestly "too much" minsan when the rules of two different worlds start to overlap in my head. Here is how I’m navigating the fragile balance between C++ and HTML/CSS.

The Logic of C++

C++ is a language I’ve learned from a young age, so it’s something I find easier to understand compared to others. However, "easy" doesn't mean it isn't sensitive; sanay na akong harapin ang mga "danas" o errors na lumalabas kapag may mali sa built-in codes ko. Even with my experience, one small mistake in the logic can break the entire program, proving that it requires a lot of mental discipline to get right.

The Maze of Design

As someone who isn't naturally a creative person, jumping into HTML/CSS felt like navigating a maze. When I once created a personal website project, I realized that web design is a lot more sensitive than C++ because of the "manual conversion" needed just to put elements in the right place. One misplaced tag or a tiny error in the CSS can ruin the whole layout, making the "basic intro" of web design feel like a heavy weight to carry.

"Lahat naman tayo nagsisimula sa umpisa... the 'halo-halo' logic of C++ prepared me to handle the strict structure of HTML/CSS."

Just like other programming languages, things look minimal and simple on the outside, but people don't see the struggles and long codes hidden behind the interface.

The Final Takeaway

At the end of the day, these challenges are worth it because I dream of making my own game in the future. The "sensitivity" of these languages isn't a barrier, but a tool that makes me a more careful and detailed developer. My plan is to keep learning both, because the more I know, the better I'll be at turning my ideas into reality.
