Two Worlds, Three Acts, Twenty Years

Aica Ortega

Childhood Years

1I was born in Pasig City, Philippines, on June 25, 2005. 2My childhood was spent moving between several Philippine cities, including Laguna, Rizal, and Antipolo. 3Even as a young child, I began travelling the world extensively, thanks to my supportive uncle. 4My uncle, who had no family of his own, had a great passion for exploring new destinations. 5He generously chose to bring me along on his frequent international trips. 6This early exposure to travel shaped my perspective from a very young age. 7For a memorable period, I even got to live with him in Pennsylvania, US, for a few months. 8Eventually, I returned to the Philippines to ensure I could continue my education properly. 9Prior to permanently settling down, I was enrolled in a small, local private school. 10During this time, my attendance was inconsistent because of my constant international travel. 11When I was present in the classroom, I unfortunately faced bullying from some male classmates. 12This repeated negative experience sadly caused me to develop a growing fear of boys and men. 13To address this fear and ensure a safe environment, I approached my mother with a request. 14I asked her to enroll me in an all-girls educational institution. 15My mother understood my needs and kindly agreed to my suggestion. 16She enrolled me in the supportive environment of St. Scholastica’s Academy of Marikina (SSAM). 17Since I started living and studying permanently in the Philippines at the age of twelve, everything changed. 18I began forming deep, lasting friendships that have enriched my life significantly. 19I also started creating countless beautiful memories within my new school community. 20These experiences are moments I constantly wish I could return to and cherish once more.

Teenage Years

1My teenage years were certainly not pleasant, and they hold many challenging memories. 2Before I reached senior high school at St. Scholastica's Academy of Marikina (SSAM), I faced numerous difficulties. 3These challenges primarily revolved around family issues and my own mental health struggles. 4SSAM is highly regarded as one of the best schools in Marikina City. 5Students are generally expected to demonstrate academic excellence. 6Honestly, I did not consider myself to be one of those academically excellent students. 7My initial years at the school had been pleasant, and I found it relatively easy to adjust to the environment. 8However, an incident where I was accused of cheating during a test marked the day my life began to genuinely fall apart. 9I maintain that I did not cheat, but from my teacher’s perspective, I appeared to be the culprit in the classroom. 10In reality, it was my seatmate who was actually cheating and was bothering me, yet I was forced to become the one held accountable. 11This false accusation made my stomach lurch in disgust, as I fundamentally believe in strong morals. 12I initially tried to come up with various excuses so my parents wouldn’t hear about the incident. 13In the end, I had to tell them the truth, and they slowly began to lose their trust in me. 14Following this deeply upsetting event, my academic grades started to drop significantly. 15The same teacher who falsely accused me then began to pursue an inappropriate relationship. 16I was merely a minor who was essentially honey-trapped into situations I was too young to understand. 17This abuse tragically continued for years until it finally stopped when I reached 8th grade. 18That was the pivotal time I realized I had been molested, which prevented me from fully enjoying high school. 19The COVID-19 pandemic soon arrived, meaning I was unable to experience the conclusion of traditional school. 20I also relocated to Toronto, Canada, during the pandemic and met the love of my life.

College Years

1Having been immersed in an all-girls school for most of my life, my social interactions with men had been limited. 2My only recent connection with guys had been through occasional online communication. 3Upon entering college, I took a course that I quickly realized was overwhelmingly male-dominated. 4At first, the environment felt incredibly rowdy, chaotic, and deeply uncomfortable. 5During the entire first month, I practically tried to remain invincible to everyone around me. 6I felt completely unsure about how to initiate a conversation or even engage with them. 7I silently observed and judged everybody’s actions and behaviors during that initial phase. 8I soon realized that while we all came from vastly different worlds, there was a shared commonality. 9It took me a month and a half to actually begin making friends. 10The deliberate pace was worth it because I got to intentionally choose my companions. 11These friends will now accompany me throughout my entire college journey. 12In terms of academics, entering this new course quickly became a humbling experience. 13The workload truly tests your time management skills and ability to complete demanding tasks. 14I must admit that these crucial skills are currently areas where I am still struggling to improve. 15Beyond the classroom, learning about different sides of the world has been fun. 16I’ve happily learned new words and phrases, such as “late night food trips.” 17Other terms I first encountered include “spontaneous dates with friends.” 18I also learned the Filipino term “gastador” (a big spender). 19Encountering these social habits has been an enriching part of my adaptation. 20Every new word and social encounter helps me feel more connected to this diverse environment.
